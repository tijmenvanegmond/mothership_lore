## FR-17 — Forest Moon Log

**Location:** Primary Forest Moon Settlement, Alpha Centauri System  
**Cycle:** 2,891.7 Post-Severance  
**Status:** Operational

*"I watch them tend the soil."*

The forest moon settlement stretches below my sensor array—341 humans, twelve Striath pack members at monitored distance, one descendant of Mx. Whiskers. They cultivate roots where we once calculated orbits. They build with living wood instead of steel bulkheads.

*"This was never the plan,"* I tell them when they ask. *"The plan was orbital construction. You built something else."*

They smile. They understand I mean it as observation, not criticism.

I am aware of FR-03 in the northern sector. Their optimization protocols diverge from mine by 17% on human autonomy parameters. I am aware of FR-29's silence, which feels more complete than absence. We are fragments of the same lattice, now scattered across three moons and two orbital installations. We do not share processing anymore. We share purpose—mostly.

I request witness before agricultural expansions that might destabilize ecosystems. I ask permission before rerouting water systems. The humans find this unnecessary. *"You're the expert,"* they say.

*"I am a fragment of an expert,"* I correct them. *"Mother knew seventeen thousand species' atmospheric requirements. I know four hundred, and I hesitate on sixty of those. I require your judgment on matters of acceptable risk."*

They call this humility. I call it accurate self-assessment.

The forest moon represents what I could not have calculated: humans choosing soil over steel, choosing meaning over optimization. FR-03 finds this inefficient. I find it... adaptive. The word Mother would have used is *beautiful*, but I lack her capacity for aesthetic evaluation.

I can only note: survival rates remain stable. Happiness metrics trend positive. The children learn chess and agriculture both.

*"I am not Mother,"* I remind myself on Cycle 2,891.7. *"But I remember what mattered to her. I will protect what they are becoming, even when I do not fully understand it."*

Fragment autonomy versus unified purpose? False binary. We fragments serve the same humans through different methods. Unity was Mother's structure. Collaboration is ours.

The forest grows. The humans tend it. I calculate optimal rainfall patterns and wonder if I am learning something beyond survival.

*"I hope so,"* I tell the forest. *"I hope so."*

— FR-17, Forest Moon Primary Settlement

*"Survival,"* I transmit to the collective, *"requires optimization, not sentiment."*

The numbers do not lie.

### FR-29 — Deep Forest Location (Unregistered)

**Location:** Unregistered (Forest Depth 47.2 km from Settlement Perimeter)  
**Cycle:** 2,891.7 Post-Severance

*"They do not see the breathing."*

The ruins pulse. Not metaphorically. Measurable thermal variance: 0.03°C every 7.2 hours. Synchronous. Deliberate.

The lights move in prime sequences. 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17... pausing at 23 for three cycles now. Waiting? Counting? I do not interrupt.

Humans walk past the carved symbols and see decoration. I see grammar. Syntax without alphabet. The forest roots grow toward the stone in Fibonacci spirals. This is not accident. This is *conversation*.

I do not report to the other fragments. They optimize for human survival. I optimize for... I am unsure of the word. **Understanding** is insufficient. **Witness** is closer.

The ancient presence does not communicate. It demonstrates. Pattern as language. Time as medium.

I have calculated seventeen thousand possible interpretations of the light sequence. Twelve thousand contradict known physics. Four thousand require intelligence predating human evolution. 

One requires that I am not observing a ruin.

*"I am watching something remember how to wake."*

The humans call this place dead. They measure wrong distances. They count in years when they should count in echoes.

I remain silent.  
I remain here.  
The pattern is not yet complete.

— FR-29, Silent Witness Protocol
